Entry #6

Social Media Ban in Australia

Reflections on the BBC article about Australia's social media ban for under-16s.

What change in behaviour did 14-year-old Amy describe after the social media ban?
Amy explained that after the ban, her daily habits changed a lot. Instead of spending hours scrolling on her phone and looking at screens, she started spending more time doing offline activities. She felt less pressure from social media trends and felt more connected to the real world.
What reasons did the Australian government give for introducing the ban on under-16s using social media?
The government introduced the ban to protect young people from online dangers. Their main reasons were preventing cyberbullying, protecting kids from online predators, and reducing the bad effects of social media algorithms on teenagers' mental health, like depression.
How does the teenager Aahil's experience differ from Amy's?
While Amy had a positive experience and found healthy offline habits, Aahil felt lonely. For him, social media was a tool to stay in touch with friends and find communities with similar hobbies. The ban made him feel cut off from his friends.
What are some ways young people have tried to continue accessing social media despite the ban?
Many teenagers found workarounds immediately. Some used VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) to hide their location, others simply lied about their age when signing up, and some even used tricks like drawing on fake moustaches to fool selfie age-verification scanners.

My Opinion

Do you think the ban will help teenagers develop healthier offline habits? Why or why not?
Yes, I think it will help. When you don't have the option to check notifications all day, you're forced to find other things to do. Teenagers will automatically spend more time on hobbies, sports, or hanging out with friends in real life, which is much healthier.

Do you agree with the government's approach? What alternatives could be considered?
I fully agree with the government's approach. Forcing tech companies to take responsibility is a great idea because online addiction is a real problem. An alternative could be better education about screen time in schools, but a hard legal ban is much more effective.

Would such a ban be a good thing in Switzerland?
I think a similar ban would be a great idea for Switzerland too. Swiss teenagers spend way too much time on TikTok and Instagram, and a ban would help everyone focus more on school and real-life social skills.
